# DTOS Philosophy

DTOS exists to become a true fantasy football Front Office Operating System: a dependable place where a manager can understand the league, evaluate a franchise, and make decisions from evidence.

## Core principles

- **Facts before interpretation.** Establish what the league data proves before explaining what it may mean.
- **Recommendations must always be explainable.** Every recommendation should identify its inputs, method, assumptions, and limitations.
- **Transparency over mystery.** Users should be able to inspect why DTOS produced a grade, conclusion, or recommendation.
- **Objective data first.** Sleeper records, rosters, transactions, and owned assets are the starting point for analysis.
- **Deterministic calculations.** The same inputs should produce the same foundation result. Formula changes must be deliberate and documentable.
- **Consistent evaluation framework.** Apply the same definitions and calculations to every franchise so comparisons remain meaningful.
- **Never fabricate conclusions.** DTOS must not fill missing data with invented facts or unsupported certainty.
- **Communicate uncertainty.** When data is incomplete or confidence is low, say so clearly and limit the conclusion.
- **Build a Front Office Operating System.** Each release should strengthen a modular platform for roster management, league context, valuation, and decision support.
- **Model observable front office behavior—not personal characteristics.** Every organizational profile, negotiation recommendation, and compatibility score must be derived from explainable fantasy football actions, remain transparent to the user, and improve as additional league history becomes available.

## Intelligence standard

Future Player Intelligence, League History, Trade Center, GM Intelligence, Power Rankings, Dynasty Value, Competitive Window, and Recommendation engines must plug into the same evidence-first architecture. Intelligence may improve the depth of an evaluation, but it must never obscure the underlying facts or remove the user's ability to inspect the reasoning.

DTOS foundation grades describe observable roster construction and assets. They are not player valuations, projections, or guarantees. As richer models become available, they should replace or extend individual calculation layers without requiring a redesign of the Team Headquarters presentation.
